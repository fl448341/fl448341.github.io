Adelaide Counter Gambit

The Adelaide Counter Gambit is a chess opening that begins with the moves 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Bc5 4. O-O Nf6. This opening is characterized by Black’s immediate counterattack against White’s center, challenging the pawn on e4 with the knight on f6. The gambit nature of the opening comes from Black’s willingness to sacrifice the pawn on e5 in order to gain active piece play and control of the center. The Adelaide Counter Gambit is an aggressive and unbalanced opening that can lead to sharp and tactical positions, making it an interesting choice for Black players looking to take the initiative early in the game.
