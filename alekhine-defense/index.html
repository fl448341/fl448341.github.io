Chess Openings

Alekhine Defense

The Alekhine Defense is a chess opening that begins with the moves 1. e4 Nf6. This opening is named after the Russian grandmaster Alexander Alekhine, who popularized it in the 1920s. The Alekhine Defense is characterized by Black’s immediate challenge to the center, aiming to control the center indirectly and create counterattacking opportunities. After the initial moves 1. e4 Nf6, the game typically continues with 2. e5 Nd5, 3. d4 d6, and 4. c4 Nb6, where Black seeks to undermine White’s center and develop active pieces.
