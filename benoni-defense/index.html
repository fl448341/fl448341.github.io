Benoni Defense

The Benoni Defense is a chess opening that begins with the moves 1. d4 Nf6 2. c4 c5. This opening is characterized by Black’s willingness to accept a pawn weakness on the d5-square in exchange for active counterplay in the center and on the queenside. The typical continuation is 3. d5 e6, where Black aims to undermine White’s center with moves like …b5 and …a6. The Benoni is an aggressive and dynamic opening that can lead to complex and tactical positions, making it a popular choice for players seeking active and unbalanced play.
