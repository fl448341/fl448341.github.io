Bowlder Attack

In the Sicilian, if white does not open up the center with an early d2-d4, black will often want to expand on the queenside while white will want to expand on the kingside. One of black’s plans is to play an early …b5 advance. The c4 bishop is ineffective at preventing this advance, and will often have to move again. Furthermore, the pressure to the f7 pawn is nonexistent. Black’s most popular answer to the Bowdler attack is 2…e6, effectively blunting this bishop, turning it into more of a target than an asset.
