Cochrane Gambit

The Cochrane Gambit is a chess opening that begins with the moves 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Bc5 4. O-O Nf6 5. d3. This opening is named after the Scottish chess player John Cochrane, who popularized it in the 19th century. The Cochrane Gambit is an aggressive opening that aims to quickly develop the white pieces and control the center. White sacrifices a pawn in the opening with the hope of gaining a strong initiative and attacking chances. The Cochrane Gambit is considered a risky but interesting opening that can lead to complex and tactical positions.
