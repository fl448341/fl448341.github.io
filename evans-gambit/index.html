Evans Gambit

The Evans Gambit is a chess opening that begins with the moves: 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Bc5 4. b4. This opening is named after the 19th-century Welsh chess master William Davies Evans. The Evans Gambit is an aggressive opening where White sacrifices a pawn in order to gain rapid development and control of the center. The opening is characterized by White’s early pawn advance to b4, challenging Black’s control of the center. This can lead to sharp, tactical positions where both players must navigate complex lines to gain an advantage.
