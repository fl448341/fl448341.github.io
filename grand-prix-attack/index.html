Grand Prix Attack

The Grand Prix Attack is a variant of the very popular Sicilian Defense. Several move orders can be used to reach the Grand Prix Attack, but a common order might be 1. e4 c5 2. Nc3 Nc6 3. f4. Rather than opening up the center with an early d2-d4, white strives to increase his space advantage and mount the beginnings of a kingside attack. It’s no wonder that the Grand Prix Attack remains popular as an anti-sicilian weapon! White generally intends to play an early Bc4 or Bb5, developing the light bishop actively. Then white can castle short and plan to attack. Black can respond to the Grand Prix Attack by expanding on the queenside (black’s c-pawn gives them space in this sector already), or by playing a well-timed strike in the center with …d5
