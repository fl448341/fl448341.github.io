How to Counter Aggressive Openings – King’s Gambit

Here is a brief description of how to counter the aggressive King’s Gambit chess opening: The King’s Gambit (1. e4 e5 2. f4) is an aggressive opening where White sacrifices a pawn in order to quickly develop their pieces and attack the center. To counter this, Black can play the solid Falkbeer Counter-Gambit (2…d5) or the more aggressive Cunningham Defense (2…exf4). In the Falkbeer, Black accepts the pawn sacrifice but then aims to quickly develop their pieces and control the center. In the Cunningham, Black immediately strikes back in the center. Both of these defenses allow Black to neutralize White’s initiative and steer the game into more solid, positional play.
