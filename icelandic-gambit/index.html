Icelandic Gambit

The Icelandic Gambit is a chess opening that begins with the moves 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Bc5 4. O-O Nf6 5. d3. This opening is characterized by White’s early development of the knight to f3 and the bishop to c4, aiming to control the center. The Icelandic Gambit is an aggressive and unbalanced opening, where White sacrifices a pawn in exchange for rapid development and control of the center. It is named after the Icelandic chess player Friðrik Ólafsson, who popularized this opening in the 1950s.
