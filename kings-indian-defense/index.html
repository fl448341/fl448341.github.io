Chess Openings

Kings Indian Defense

The Kings Indian Defense is a popular chess opening that begins with the moves 1. d4 Nf6 2. c4 g6. This opening is characterized by Black’s fianchettoing of the king’s bishop, creating a strong pawn center for White, and a tense, strategic battle for control of the center. Black’s plan is to undermine White’s center with moves like …c5 and …Bg4, while also developing a strong kingside attack. The first 3-5 moves typically go: 1. d4 Nf6 2. c4 g6 3. Nc3 Bg7 4. e4 d6 5. Nf3.
