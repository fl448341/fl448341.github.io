Portuguese Gambit

The Portuguese Gambit is a chess opening that begins with the moves 1.e4 e5 2.Bb5 Nf6 3.d4. This opening is characterized by White's unusual bishop development to b5 combined with an immediate central pawn push, aiming to disrupt Black’s natural development and create tactical complications. White willingly offers material, typically the central pawn on e4, to quickly activate pieces and seize the initiative. The Portuguese Gambit often results in dynamic, open positions that favor players who enjoy tactical battles and are comfortable handling imbalanced positions from the very start of the game.
