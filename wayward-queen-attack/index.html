Wayward Queen Attack

The Wayward Queen Attack is a chess opening that begins with the moves 1.e4 e5 2.Qh5. This opening is characterized by White's early and unusual queen development, directly aiming at Black’s vulnerable f7-pawn and putting immediate pressure on Black’s position. Although aggressive, the early queen move violates traditional opening principles, exposing White’s queen to potential harassment. The opening relies primarily on traps and tactical oversights from Black, often leading to sharp, unbalanced positions. The Wayward Queen Attack appeals to players looking for quick tactical opportunities and the potential to surprise an unprepared opponent.
